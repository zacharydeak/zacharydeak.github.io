About

Zachary Deak is a pianist and musicologist whose work brings together performance, historical research, and the study of musical interpretation. He has appeared as a soloist and chamber musician across the United States, France, Morocco, Portugal, Moldova, and the United Kingdom, with performances at venues including the Dorothy Chandler Pavilion in Los Angeles, the Cité de la Musique and Salle Cortot in Paris, and the Casa da Música in Porto.

A graduate of the École Normale de Musique de Paris and the Royal College of Music in London, he holds a Doctor of Musical Arts in Piano Performance from the University of Southern California. He is Assistant Professor of Piano and Musicology and Director of Keyboard Studies at Old Dominion University.

Research

Deak’s research examines how musical interpretation is taught, transmitted, remembered, and transformed across institutions, cultures, and technologies. His work centers on piano pedagogy, performance practice, archival research, and the cultural history of music education.

Current Projects

  • Alfred Cortot, the École Normale de Musique, and the pedagogy of interpretation

    Research on Cortot’s cours d’interprétation, archival traces of piano teaching, and the transmission of performance practice in twentieth-century France.

  • Musical Education and Cultural Exchange in Morocco

    A study of conservatory culture, musical education, and Franco-Moroccan exchange from the Protectorate period to the present.

    ODU Summer Research Fellowship
  • AI-Assisted Analysis of Historical Piano Performance

    A multimodal research project using large corpora of historical recordings and scores to surface patterns of timing, articulation, and interpretation across pianistic traditions.

    ODU ARTnership Project
  • “Revivre le cours: Alfred Cortot, archives pédagogiques et transmission interprétative sur le Steinway 1907” Crédit Agricole d’Île-de-France Research Fellowship · Bibliothèque musicale La Grange-Fleuret, Paris · 2026

Teaching

At Old Dominion University, Deak directs keyboard studies and teaches applied piano, music history, and pedagogy. His courses include Music and Ideas, Music in the Romantic Era, Music of the 20th Century to the Present, Applied Music Literature, and Applied Music Pedagogy.

He also teaches online through ArtistWorks in the course “Piano with Zachary Deak.”

Previously, he taught at Cal State University, Los Angeles and Cuesta College in San Luis Obispo.
